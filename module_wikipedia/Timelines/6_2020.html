This article documents the chronology and epidemiology of SARS-CoV-2 in June 2020, the virus which causes the coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) and is responsible for the COVID-19 pandemic. The first human cases of COVID-19 were identified in Wuhan, China, in December 2019.

Case statistics

Further information: Timeline of the COVID-19 pandemic § Case statistics

Pandemic chronology

1 June

WHO Situation Report 133:[1]

  • Canada has reported 759 new cases, bringing the total number to 91,705.[2]
  • Malaysia has reported 38 new cases, bringing the total to 7,857. There are 1,338 active cases, with eight in intensive care and two on ventilator support. 51 patients have been discharged, bringing the number of recovered to 6,404. The death toll remains at 115.[3]
  • New Zealand has reported no new cases, recoveries, and deaths; which remain at 1,504 (1,154 confirmed and 350 probable), 1,481, and 22 respectively. There remains one active case.[4]
  • Singapore has reported 408 new cases, bringing the total to 35,292.[5] Another death was later confirmed, bringing the total to 24.[6]
  • Ukraine has reported 340 new cases and 10 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 24,012 and 718 respectively; a total of 9,690 patients have recovered.[7]
  • Nikol Pashinyan, Prime Minister of Armenia, has tested positive for COVID-19.[citation needed]

2 June

WHO Situation Report 134:[8]

  • Canada has reported 705 new cases, bringing the total number to 92,410.[9]
  • Malaysia has reported 20 new cases (15 imported and five locally transmitted), bringing the total number to 7,877. 66 patients have recovered, bringing the total number to 6,470. There are 1,292 active cases; six in intensive care and two on ventilator support. The death toll has remained at 115.[10]
  • New Zealand has reported no new cases, recoveries, and deaths; which remain at 1,504 (1,154 confirmed and 350 probable), 1,481, and 22 respectively. There remains one active case.[11] Director-General of Health Ashley Bloomfield confirmed that 654 tests were completed on Monday, bringing the total number of tests to 282,263.[12]
  • Singapore has reported 544 new cases, bringing the total to 35,836.[13][14]
  • Ukraine has reported 328 new cases and 9 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 24,340 and 727 respectively; a total of 10,078 patients have recovered.[15]

  • Malaysia has reported 93 new cases, bringing the total number to 7,970. There are 1,324 active cases; six in intensive care and two on ventilator support. 61 patients have recovered, bringing the total number of recovered to 6,531. The death toll remains at 115.[18]

  • Ukraine has reported record 588 new cases as well as 12 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 25,411 and 747 respectively; a total of 11,042 patients have recovered.[29]

  • The United States has reported a total of 2,000,464 cases and 112,908 deaths.[74]

  • Singapore has reported 463 new cases, bringing the total to 39,850.[87][88]

  • China has reported 40 new cases, including 27 in Beijing. The capital has reported a total of 106 cases.[114]
  • Malaysia has reported 11 new cases (10 local transmissions and one imported), bringing the total to 8,505. There have been 333 recoveries, bringing the total number of recovered patients to 7,733. The death toll remains at 121.[115]

The total number of recovered and deaths remain at 1,482 and 22 respectively.[116] These two new cases break the 24 consecutive days of no new cases in the country.[117]
  • Singapore has reported 151 new cases, bringing the total to 40,969.[118][119]
  • Ukraine has reported 666 new cases and 11 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 32,476 and 912 respectively; a total of 14,528 patients have recovered.[120]

17 June

WHO Situation Report 149:[121]

  • Canada has reported 386 new cases, bringing the total number to 99,853.[122][123]
  • Malaysia has reported ten new cases (all local transmissions) bringing the total to 8,515. There are 642 active aces with four in intensive care. 140 patients have been discharged, bringing the total number of recoveries to 7,873. The death toll remains at 121.[124]

The total number of recovered and deaths remain at 1,482 and 22 respectively.[116] That same day, the Ministry of Health announced that it had identified 320 people who had been in contact with the two new cases.[125][126]
  • Singapore has reported 247 new cases, bringing the total to 41,216.[127]
  • Ukraine sets a new record for both daily cases and deaths at 758 and 31 respectively. The totals are now 33,234 cases and 943 lethal outcomes. Additionally, 14,943 patients have recovered overall.[128]

18 June

WHO Situation Report 150:[129]

  • Canada has reported 367 new cases, surpassing 100,000 cases bringing the total number to 100,220.[130]
  • Malaysia has reported 14 new cases, bringing the total to 8,529. There are 408 active cases, with four in intensive care. 127 have recovered, bringing the total number of recoveries to 8,000. The death toll remains at 119.[131][132]
  • New Zealand has reported one new case, bringing the number of active cases to three and the total number to 1,507 (1,157 confirmed and 350 probable).[133] The new case is a man in his sixties who traveled from Pakistan to Doha to Melbourne on 11 June before flying from Melbourne to Auckland on 13 June.[134] A total of 327,460 people have been tested in New Zealand, with 6,273 tested on 18 June.[135]
  • Singapore has reported 257 new cases, bringing the total to 41,473.[136][137]
  • Ukraine sets a new record for daily cases at 829 and reports 23 new deaths. The totals are now 34,063 cases and 966 lethal outcomes. Additionally, 15,447 patients have recovered overall.[138]
  • Nursultan Nazarbayev, former President of Kazakhstan, has tested positive for COVID-19.[citation needed]

19 June

WHO Situation Report 151:[139]

  • Brazil reaches 1 million cases worldwide.[140]
  • Canada has reported 409 new cases the highest daily case count since June 13 2020, bringing the total number to 100,629.[141]
  • Malaysia has reported six new cases, bringing the total number to 8,535. There are 344 active cases with four in intensive care. 70 have recovered, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,070. The death toll remains at 121.[142] That same day, the Director-General Noor Hisham Abdullah confirmed the discovery of a new cluster in Kidurong, Sarawak.[143]
  • New Zealand has reported no new cases, with the total number of active cases remaining at three. A total of 327,460 people have been tested in New Zealand.[135] The total number of cases remains 1,507 (1,157 confirmed and 350 probable).[144]
  • Singapore has reported 142 new cases, bringing the total to 41,615.[145][146]
  • Ukraine sets a new record for daily cases at 921 and reports 19 new deaths. The totals are now 34,984 cases and 985 lethal outcomes. Additionally, 16,033 patients have recovered overall.[147]

20 June

21 June

25 June

WHO Situation Report 157:[190]
