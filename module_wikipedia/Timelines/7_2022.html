This article documents the chronology and epidemiology of SARS-CoV-2, the virus that causes the coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) and is responsible for the COVID-19 pandemic, in July 2022. The first human cases of COVID-19 were identified in Wuhan, China, in December 2019.

Case statistics[edit]

Further information: Timeline of the COVID-19 pandemic § Case statistics

Pandemic chronology[edit]

1 July[edit]

  • Chile surpasses 4 million COVID-19 cases.[citation needed]
  • Malaysia has reported 2,773 new cases, bringing the total number to 4,568,828. There are 1,984 recoveries, bringing the total number of recoveries to 4,502,840. There are six deaths, bringing the death toll to 35,771.[1]
  • New Zealand has reported 7,195 new cases, bringing the total number to 1,345,796. There are 5,015 recoveries, bringing the total number of recoveries to 1,298,282. There are seven deaths, bringing the death toll to 1,529.[2]
  • Niue has reported five new cases resulting from overseas travel.[3]
  • North Korea has reported 4,570 new cases, bringing the total number to 4,744,430. The death toll stands at 73.[4]
  • Singapore has reported 9,087 new cases, bringing the total number to 1,453,155. Two new deaths were reported, bringing the death toll to 1,415.[5]
  • Taiwan reports 35,800 new cases, bringing the total number to 3,803,049. 121 new deaths were reported, bringing the death toll to 6,772.[6]
  • Sri Lankan cricketer Angelo Mathews has tested positive for COVID-19 and was ruled out of Sri Lanka's first test against Australia.[7]
